# PERFORMANCE WORK STATEMENT (PWS)
## Cybersecurity & IT Sustainment Subcontract

**Prime Contractor:** Lockheed Martin Aeronautics
**Subcontractor:** Ironclad Defense Solutions, Inc.
**End Customer:** U.S. Air Force, Air Combat Command
**Subcontract No.:** LM-ACC-2026-4471
**Period of Performance:** 12 months, with four 12-month option periods

---

### 1. Background and Scope

Ironclad Defense Solutions, Inc. ("the Subcontractor") shall provide cybersecurity sustainment, IT operations, and secure software support for the [Program Name] weapons-system support environment. Work is performed at the Subcontractor's two facilities and will involve the receipt, storage, processing, and transmission of **Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)**, including Controlled Technical Information (CTI) and export-controlled data subject to ITAR.

The environment supports approximately **180 users** across **2 sites**, operating on **Microsoft GCC High**. Support is provided during **standard business hours** with on-call coverage for security incidents.

### 2. Applicable Requirements

The Subcontractor shall comply with:
- **DFARS 252.204-7012** — Safeguarding Covered Defense Information and Cyber Incident Reporting.
- **DFARS 252.204-7019 / 7020** — NIST SP 800-171 assessment and SPRS reporting.
- **DFARS 252.204-7021** — Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (**CMMC Level 2**) as a condition of award.
- **NIST SP 800-171 Rev. 2** — all 110 security requirements.
- **32 CFR Part 2002** — CUI marking and handling.

### 3. Performance Requirements

3.1 The Subcontractor shall implement and maintain all 110 NIST SP 800-171 security controls across the 14 control families and shall achieve **CMMC Level 2 certification** by a C3PAO prior to the end of the base period.

3.2 The Subcontractor shall maintain a current **System Security Plan (SSP)** and **Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M)**, and shall report its SPRS score.

3.3 The Subcontractor shall report cyber incidents affecting CUI to the DoD within **72 hours** via DIBNet, and shall preserve affected media and images for at least 90 days.

3.4 The Subcontractor shall enforce **multifactor authentication** for privileged and remote access, **FIPS-validated cryptography** for CUI at rest and in transit, and least-privilege access control.

3.5 The Subcontractor shall provide annual security awareness and insider-threat training to all personnel with access to CUI.

3.6 The Subcontractor shall flow down these requirements to any lower-tier subcontractors handling CUI.

### 4. Service Levels

| Requirement | Target |
|---|---|
| Critical security patch remediation | Within 30 days |
| Cyber incident reporting to DoD | Within 72 hours |
| Access review | Quarterly |
| Vulnerability scanning | Monthly |
| CMMC Level 2 certification | Before end of base period |

### 5. Deliverables

- System Security Plan (SSP) and POA&M
- CUI asset inventory and network/data-flow diagram
- Incident response plan and evidence of annual testing
- Training completion records
- Monthly security status report

### 6. Data Rights and Handling

All CUI remains the property of the U.S. Government. The Subcontractor shall not process CUI on any system or service that is not authorized to the appropriate level, and shall use cloud services meeting FedRAMP Moderate (or DoD equivalent) where CUI is processed.
